## Findings

### D-1 — CRITICAL / Variant A, first sentence / unsupported `[VERIFIED]` citation

> "Digital Coop Bank is designed to operate as a directly licensed Savings and Credit
> Cooperative (SCC) under the FRC **[VERIFIED: CLAUDE.md]**"

`CLAUDE.md` does not support this. It supports only the *regulator mapping* ("FDIC/NCUA →
**FRC** … for cooperatives"). On the actual question — whether this platform is an SCC —
`CLAUDE.md` blocking question #1 says the opposite:

> "**Common bond (SCC Law Art. 19.3)** — members must share an employer, association, or
> aimag/district. **A nationwide open-membership app may not be a lawful SCC.** This bounds
> the entire product."

So the marker asserts source support for a claim the cited source flags as unresolved and
blocking. Given this project's history of a fabricated licensed-entity register, a
`[VERIFIED]` marker that does not survive spot-check is the highest-severity class of
defect here, even though the underlying source is a local file rather than an invention.

**Correct fix:** delete the SCC-is-decided assertion from Variant A, or restate it as a
candidate ("one of two structures under counsel review"), which collapses Variant A into
Variant B. If Variant A is to remain meaningfully "direct", the most it may assert is the
*negative* that is actually established — the US sponsor-bank/BaaS framing does not apply —
with the positive entity form left open.

### D-2 — CRITICAL / Variant A / decides an open question in a normative document

Independent of the citation, Variant A reads as if the charter were settled. Judged against
its own stated purpose ("states the SCC framing directly"), directness is legitimate;
*deciding counsel's question* is not. It contradicts three existing records that the branch
itself cites approvingly:

- `01_business_analysis.md` §6.3 item 3 — "depends on the charter obtained (credit union vs
  bank vs e-money partnership)"
- `05_prd_and_roadmap.md` OI-3 — "No member-facing insurance claim ships before the charter
  is fixed"
- `05_prd_and_roadmap.md` R-1 — "the charter route … shifts compliance obligations"

A variant cannot simultaneously be "written to remain consistent with those open items
staying open" (the author's words) and assert the answer. **Fix:** as D-1.

### D-3 — HIGH / both variants / `[VERIFIED: …]` markers inside the paste-ready text

`[VERIFIED: mongolbank.mn/en/p/1305]` and `[VERIFIED: CLAUDE.md]` sit *inside* the strings a
downstream task pastes into `04_technical_architecture.md`. That document contains **zero**
provenance markers today — its three `VERIFIED` hits are all unrelated (an
`ExternalAccountLink` status enum at lines 273/1070 and an unrelated `verified contact
channel` at 757). Pasting these injects handoff review metadata into the baseline.

**Correct fix:** strip all `[VERIFIED: …]` markers from the four paste blocks; keep the
provenance in the handoff's Sources section where it belongs. Mechanical, ~4 lines.

### D-4 — MEDIUM / Variant A / narrowed insurance denial

> "Member deposits held at the cooperative are **not** insured or guaranteed **by this
> arrangement**"

The non-negotiable is not violated — no variant describes savings as insured, protected or
guaranteed, and neither quotes or paraphrases SCC Law Art. 55.1's phantom promise in any
form. **The insurance non-negotiable passes in both variants.** But the qualifier "by this
arrangement" narrows the denial to the settlement-agent relationship and by implication
leaves open that coverage exists elsewhere. Against a statute that promises SCC insurance in
Art. 55.1 while the operative Law on Insurance of Bank Deposits covers banks only, a narrowed
denial is exactly the wording that could be mined for member-facing copy.

**Correct fix:** flat denial or none. "Member deposits at the cooperative are not covered by
deposit insurance; see OI-3." Variant B's approach — make no insurance claim in either
direction and point to OI-3 — is safer still.

### D-5 — MEDIUM / both variants / naming five banks is scope creep and staleness risk

Both variants enumerate XacBank, Golomt Bank, Trade and Development Bank, Arig Bank and
M Bank inline. The citation itself **holds** (see Provenance below) — this is not a
fabrication finding. But the task scope was "this one line and its dependent sentences";
vendor and entity strategy are separate work, and a licensing register is precisely the kind
of Governor-order-volatile list that `CLAUDE.md` says to treat as configuration rather than
hard-code. Naming five counterparties in a requirements baseline commits a procurement
decision the same sentence says is not a requirements change.

**Correct fix (recommendation, not a change I am making):** cite the register by reference —
"a bank on Bank of Mongolia's settlement-agent register" — and leave the enumeration in the
handoff.

### D-6 — MEDIUM / both variants / the resulting table row contradicts itself

Both variants leave the second column verbatim: "FBO account structure, ACH
origination/receipt, wire, real-time rails (FedNow), settlement of card network activity."
Variant B's own first column then says settlement "will run through a Bank-of-Mongolia-licensed
bank". After paste, one table row asserts both Mongolian settlement and FedNow/ACH/wire.

Leaving the rails alone is correct scoping and the author flagged it. But the *deliverable*
is a paste-ready row, and the pasted row is internally inconsistent.

**Correct fix:** append an explicit in-document marker to the second column, e.g.
`<!-- TODO(rails): US rails — replace with RTGS/Banksuljee, ACH+, NETC. Separate backlog item. -->`
so the contradiction is visibly owned rather than silently shipped.

### D-7 — LOW / both variants / row rename creates a dangling cross-reference

Renaming the row label to "Settlement-agent / payment-licensed bank" leaves
`04` §4.3 still titled "Sponsor Bank / BaaS — Money Rails" (line 1074) and `05` R-2 still
naming "BaaS / sponsor-bank dependency … (`04` §4.3)". Renaming §4.3 is out of scope, so the
rename half-lands. The author's **fallback parentheticals — which keep the label
"Sponsor bank / BaaS provider" — avoid this and are the better of the two forms offered.**
