## Blockers

**The gate does not reach PASS with Variant B applied verbatim, and I did not improvise a fix.**
Two independent, mechanical reasons, both confirmed by direct measurement, not assumption:

1. **FDIC/NCUA HARD check stays at 2, not 0.** `.softhouse/verify-docs.sh`'s check is a bare
   case-insensitive substring match: `fdic|ncua|...`. It cannot distinguish "asserting FDIC/NCUA
   coverage" from "naming the FDIC/NCUA framing that is being withdrawn." Variant B's ratified
   text reads "*The previous **FDIC/NCUA** framing assumed a US sponsor-bank/BaaS structure that
   does not apply in Mongolia and is withdrawn*" — one `FDIC`, one `NCUA`, exactly the same count
   as the row it replaces (`FDIC/NCUA-insured partner institution` — also one of each). Applying
   Variant B is therefore a wash on this counter, not a regression, but it is not the "reaches 0"
   result the task instructions anticipated. `[VERIFIED: grep -noiE '\bfdic\b|\bncua\b' on
   line 29, before and after — 1 FDIC + 1 NCUA in both cases]`
2. **Rails DRIFT rises from 56 to 59 (+3), over the `.softhouse/baseline.txt` ceiling of 56.**
   The verifier's rails check is `\b(ACH|FedNow|SEPA)\b`, and it counts every mention regardless
   of context — including inside a `<!-- TODO -->` comment that exists specifically to *name*
   the problem for a later backlog item. The pre-edit row already contributed 2 hits (`ACH`,
   `FedNow` in "ACH origination/receipt, wire, real-time rails (FedNow)" — unchanged by Variant B).
   Variant B's ratified `TODO(rails)` comment adds 3 more inside the same row: "ACH, FedNow and
   wire do not exist in Mongolia... **ACH+**" — `ACH` and `FedNow` are repeated, and `ACH+` also
   matches `\bACH\b` (the `+` is a non-word character, so the boundary matches). Net: row goes
   from contributing 2 to contributing 5, total file-wide rails count 56 → 59.
   `[VERIFIED: grep -noE '\b(ACH|FedNow|SEPA)\b' idea-lab/final_requirements/04_technical_architecture.md,
   before/after, plus full verify-docs.sh run confirming 59 (+3 over baseline 56)]`
