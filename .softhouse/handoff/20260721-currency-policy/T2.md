**Note A — the 78-token set is not a clean set of 78 dollar amounts. Two caveats the apply task MUST heed:**

- **Comma artifacts (2 tokens):** `$5,` and `$10,` (both `04:104`) are truncations of `$5` and `$10`
  inside a comma-separated list cell (`"$10, multi-share holdings"` / `"$5, one share per member"`).
  They are NOT the amounts `$5,xxx`/`$10,xxx`; collapse them into `$5` and `$10`.
  → 78 tokens − 2 artifacts = **76 genuine distinct amounts**.
- **Magnitude-suffix truncations (bare integers):** the regex stops at `k`/`M`, so several bare-integer
  tokens are actually thousands/millions figures. These are NOT small-dollar amounts:
  - `$55`→$55k, `$90`→$90k, `$35`→$35k, `$60`(01:47)→$60k, `$45`→$45k, `$70`→$70k  (persona **annual incomes**)

  - `$1`(05:189 context)→`$1.5M`; `$100`(05:187)→`$100k` ceiling; `$500`(05:189)→flat `$500` cap prose
  Any pass that blindly converts the bare integer will corrupt these. They are individually categorised below.

## 4. Flags

### 4a. Genuinely ambiguous / polysemous tokens (per-line categorisation is mandatory)
- **`$25.00` / `$25`** — SHARE_PRICE **and** coincidental P2P amounts (03:487,489,507,1495) **and** KPIs ($25M AUM 01:113; CPA 01:107). Miscoupling the P2P `$25.00`s to the par primitive would wrongly recompute them.
- **`$1,000.00` / `$1,000`** — three roles: AML step-up threshold (DEC-36), de-ratified loan cap (DEC-45, superseded), community pitch min-goal (DEC-63), plus interest worked-check principal. Split by line.
- **`$5,000.00`** — loan max (DEC-45) **and** per-project surplus-match cap (US-9.3).
- **`$5.00` / `$5`** — Round-Up batch threshold (DEC-67) **and** rejected $5 share par (superseded) **and** superseded round-up threshold.
- **`$10,000` / `$10,000.00`** — DEC-73 AML monitoring seed **and** DEC-65 match-cap ceiling.
- **`$500.00` / `$500`** — DEC-65 superseded flat cap **and** many unrelated scenario amounts.
- **`$100.00`** (20 occ) / **`$50.00`** (15 occ) / **`$200.00`** (15 occ) — high-frequency scenario amounts spanning P2P, loans, backings, pledges; a few are primitives (loan min $100.00). Treat per §3 blocks; do not bulk-convert without checking each line's equation.
- **Bare integers** are magnitude-truncated (Note A) — never convert the bare integer in isolation.
- **FEE-$0.00 vs ZERO-$0.00** — ambiguous but immaterial (both → MNT 0).

### 4b. Amounts touching an OPEN decision
- **DEC-11 share-price legality (Open Item 1 / OI-1):** `$25.00` par is "the working canonical value … subject to legal/regulatory confirmation" (01:367,410 · 05:382). The keystone is not legally final. Every WORKED_EXAMPLE in §3.A depends on it (`$84.60`, `$9.60`, `939 × $25.00`).
- **Loan rate model (DEC-48 12% base / DEC-49 tiers / DEC-52 grace):** the `$88.20` installment (§3.J) is an amortization output of principal × rate × term. Until the rate model is confirmed and the amortization convention (ACT/365F, 06:357) is applied, `$88.20` cannot be re-derived — it is coupled to the rate primitive, not scalable. **Flag `$88.20` as blocked on the rate model.**
- **Share-capital pledge enforceability (Open Item 2 / LA-9):** governs whether pledge `$` examples (§3.J) touch share capital; mechanics behind a jurisdiction flag. Affects narrative, not the arithmetic values.
- **Subchapter-T governance-factor legality (LA-1, 06 §):** affects dividend weights (percentages), not the `$` amounts directly.

### 4c. KPI set HELD OUT of the currency pass (income-independent — rebaseline separately)
`$25M`/`$150M` AUM (KPI-2.1), `$1,500` avg deposit (KPI-2.2), `$25` CPA (KPI-1.4), `$1.5M` community
capital (KPI-4.4), `$25,000,000.00` AUM illustration (03:1806). These scale with **sector/market size**,
not member income — do not convert them in the income pass.

### 4d. Persona incomes are the rebasing BASIS, not targets
`$55k/$90k/$35k/$60k/$45k/$70k` (01:19,47,61) are the USD incomes the whole migration re-anchors
against. They are not product amounts; the income-basis task (not the generic currency pass) should own them.
