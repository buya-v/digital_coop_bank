## Method recap (why these numbers are ~6–8× below a naive conversion, and that is CORRECT)

The Mongolian **median monthly wage is MNT 2,278,400 ≈ USD 636** at the cross-check rate (USD/MNT
≈ 3,580). The US design's implied income basis is far higher, so **income-anchored limits
re-derived against local wages land ~6–8× below what `USD × 3,580` would produce.** That gap is
the whole point of re-deriving instead of converting: a naive conversion would preserve a
dollar-sized limit that is enormous relative to Mongolian incomes. The USD/MNT rate is used **only**
to sanity-check that a re-derived figure sits in a sane range — never as the derivation input.

**Primary anchor:** `W = median monthly wage = MNT 2,278,400` (T1 anchor 1b, VERIFIED number,
medium confidence on the thousand-MNT *unit* inference — T1 Decision D1; if that unit is wrong every
income-anchored row scales by 1000×, so this caveat rides on all such rows).
**Secondary corroboration anchors:** avg wage MNT 2,726,900 (1a); SCC cumulative paid-in capital
≈ MNT 399,000/member (anchor 4, NOT a share price); typical NBFI outstanding loan balance
≈ MNT 2,240,000/borrower (anchor 3, proxy).

**Rounding rule (applied consistently):**
- Small primitives (share par, round-up denomination): **nearest ₮1,000**.
- Limits / bounds / thresholds: **nearest ₮50,000**.
- All figures **whole tugrik** (möngö obsolete — T1 whole-tugrik note). No decimal-tugrik precision.

- **RULE (proposed):** set par `P` as a small, affordable, whole-tugrik buy-in — a similar tiny

  cumulative-capital benchmark.

  (a) ≈ 0.44% of median monthly wage W — a single share stays affordable for the lowest-income

  (c) clean tugrik, ends in ₮10,000 for mental math and UX. Sanity check: naive `$25 × 3,580 =
  MNT 89,500`; the re-derived ₮10,000 is deliberately lower, anchored to local affordability.
- **Coupling:** every WORKED_EXAMPLE in §A below (`$84.60`, `$9.60`, `939 × $25`, `$75`) inherits

## Decisions the PO must confirm (the real anchor choices — approve/adjust these, not 76 rows)

1. **Membership share par `P`** — *PENDING-ANCHOR (DEC-11 / OI-1, PO + legal).*
   **Recommend `P = MNT 10,000/share`.** No authoritative single-share benchmark exists (the ₮399k
   T1 figure is cumulative, not per-share); ₮10,000 is ≈0.44% of median monthly wage, divides the
   cumulative-capital benchmark into ~40 lifetime shares, and is clean tugrik. Keystone — everything
   in §A stays PENDING until this is set.

2. **Median-wage multiples for AML limits** — **Recommend step-up = 0.25·W → ₮550,000; P2P daily
   velocity = 0.50·W → ₮1,150,000.** Preserves the original $1k:$2k design ratio, re-anchored to the
   Mongolian median wage; naive conversion (~₮3.6M / ₮7.2M) is ~6× too high for local incomes.

3. **Median-wage multiples for loan bounds** — **Recommend min = 0.05·W → ₮100,000; max = 2.5·W →
   ₮5,700,000.** These bracket the real NBFI average outstanding loan (₮2.24M) — strong
   corroboration that the multiples are sane.

4. **AML aggregate-outbound monitoring threshold** — **Recommend 5·W ≈ ₮11,500,000** (cleaner round
   alternative ₮10,000,000). Preserves the $10k design ratio; compliance owns the production rule.

5. **Round-up denomination + batch threshold** — **Recommend round up to nearest ₮1,000; batch
   settle at ≈₮15,000.** The "nearest USD" mechanic has no clean MNT equivalent (T2 flag); ₮1,000 is
   the smallest note-friendly clean unit. This is its own sub-decision — not silently picked.

6. **Program-config caps (DEC-65 surplus-match ceiling; DEC-63 pitch goal bounds)** — *PENDING
   (program-budget decision, likely held with the KPI cluster).* **Recommend deferring** and
   re-deriving proportionally once the program budget is set — these track program size, not member
   income.
