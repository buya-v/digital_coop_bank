## Findings (severity / location / what / fix)

### F1 — LOW-MEDIUM · T3 income-ladder row `$10,000` (DEC-73) **and** PO decision #4 · rounding rule not applied → wrong number, propagated to the PO list
- **What.** The stated rule is "limits/bounds/thresholds → nearest ₮50,000", and the row's rounding
  column says exactly that. But `5 × W = 5 × 2,278,400 = 11,392,000`, and **11,392,000 rounded to
  the nearest ₮50,000 is ₮11,400,000, not ₮11,500,000.** (11,392,000 / 50,000 = 227.84 → 228 →
  11,400,000.) T3 proposes **₮11,500,000**, which is off the ₮50,000 grid, and repeats ₮11,500,000
  in **PO decision #4**. The "cleaner alt ₮10,000,000" parenthetical shows the analyst was eyeballing
  round numbers here rather than applying the row's own rule — every *other* limit in the ladder was
  rounded correctly (I re-verified all five; see F-method below), so this one is an outlier, not a
  policy.
- **Why it matters.** It is the single instance where a concrete proposed MNT figure does not follow
  from its stated multiple + rounding rule, and it reaches the PO as an approvable number. A PO
  approving "5·W, nearest ₮50,000" would be signing off ₮11,500,000 that the rule does not produce.
- **Fix.** Change the proposed value and PO decision #4 to **₮11,400,000** to obey the stated rule.
  *Alternatively*, if a deliberately-rounder AML threshold is wanted (the confidence note says
  "compliance owns the production rule"), keep ₮11,500,000 or ₮10,000,000 **but relabel that row's
  rounding as an explicit exception** ("rounded to nearest ₮500k / ₮1M for a clean compliance
  threshold") so the number is honest about not following the ₮50,000 rule. Either is fine; the
  current state — a ₮50,000 label on a non-₮50,000 number — is not.

### F2 — LOW · WORKED_EXAMPLES table, block-coupled `$6,000` illustrative row · illustrative figure off the ₮50,000 grid
- **What.** `1.2 × loan-max = 1.2 × 5,700,000 = 6,840,000`; under nearest-₮50,000 this is ₮6,850,000.
  T3 shows **₮6,840,000**. This is explicitly labelled "illustrative" and is a coupled recompute
  (correct method — do not scale $6,000), so it is not a substantive error, but it is inconsistent
  with the rounding rule the same table applies elsewhere.
- **Fix.** Optional: note that the illustrative figure is shown unrounded, or round to ₮6,850,000 for
  consistency. Not blocking.

### F3 — LOW · Round-up batch threshold `$5.00 → ₮15,000` (row + PO decision #5) · denomination-to-batch ratio silently changed
- **What.** In the US design the round-up denomination is $1 (nearest whole dollar) and the batch
  threshold is $5 — a **5×** ratio. T3 recommends denomination ₮1,000 and batch ₮15,000 — a **15×**
  ratio. The 5× design relationship is not preserved and no reason is given (₮15,000 ≈ $5 × 3,580,
  i.e. it looks like a spot-conversion of the $5, whereas the denomination was re-derived). This is
  correctly surfaced as a PO sub-decision, so it is not a hidden defect, but the internal ratio shift
  is worth flagging to the PO alongside it.
- **Fix.** Either justify the 15× (e.g. batch less often because ₮1,000 round-ups accrue fast) or
  note ₮5,000 as the ratio-preserving alternative, so the PO chooses knowingly.

### F-observation (not a defect) — headline "~6–8× below naive conversion" is imprecise
The method recap claims income-anchored limits land ~6–8× below `USD × 3,580`. I recomputed: step-up
and velocity are ~6.2–6.5×, but the loan bounds and the AML aggregate are only ~3.1–3.6× (the row's
own sanity line correctly says "~3–6×"). The headline overstates the general factor. Numbers are all
disclosed per-row so nothing is hidden; just tighten the summary to "~3–6.5×" if desired.

## The eight checks

**1. Load-bearing unit caveat — is the ₮2.28M/month anchor safe to build on? YES, safe.**
I cross-checked the thousand-MNT inference three independent ways and it holds decisively:
- **Everyday prices reconcile.** Median wage ₮2,278,400/month buys ~526 canteen meals (₮4,335) or
  ~780 loaves (₮2,920). Plausible for a monthly wage. The *alternative* reading (raw MNT, i.e.
  ₮2,278/month) would be **half of one canteen lunch per month** — absurd. The price points falsify
  the non-thousand reading.
- **USD cross-check reconciles.** ₮2,278,400 / 3,580 = **USD 636/month** (T1's figure exactly).
- **GDP per capita reconciles.** World Bank GDP/capita USD 7,108/yr ≈ USD 592/month; a median
  *employee* wage of USD 636/month sits just above it, exactly as expected. A 1000×-off wage
  (USD 0.64/mo or USD 636k/mo) would be wildly inconsistent with GDP/capita.
  **Conclusion:** the anchor is safe. T3 honestly carries the caveat as "medium confidence" and rides
  it on every income row, which is the right posture, but the reviewer's cross-check actually resolves
  it *stronger than medium* — the alternative is not merely unlikely, it is arithmetically impossible
  against T1's own price data. The 1000× catastrophe is not a live risk. (It remains worth a one-line
  confirmation from an NSO unit label before legal ratification, per T1 D1 — but the table does not
  "rest on an unconfirmed unit" in any real sense.)
